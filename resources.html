Essential Books

Read one of these and you'll understand the humanoid moment better than most people in the room.

❤️The Heart and the Chip

By Daniela Rus, Director of MIT's Computer Science and AI Lab. The single best book on robotics right now — humane, authoritative, and written by someone who is actually building these systems.

⭐ #1 RecommendationRead it →
🌊The Coming Wave

By Mustafa Suleyman (DeepMind co-founder). The clearest big-picture frame for understanding AI and robotics together — what's coming, what it means for society, and what we can actually do.

Big Picture · SocietyRead it →
⚖️Power and Progress

By MIT economists Daron Acemoglu and Simon Johnson. A rigorous look at who benefits from automation — and who gets left behind. Essential for understanding the labor implications of humanoid robots.

Economics · LaborRead it →
🤝Co-Intelligence

By Wharton Professor Ethan Mollick. Practical and optimistic — shows how humans and AI can work together rather than compete. The most readable entry point into human-AI collaboration.

Practical · OptimisticRead it →
🐍AI Snake Oil

By Arvind Narayanan and Sayash Kapoor. A healthy counterweight to the hype — separates genuine AI progress from marketing fiction. Sharpens your ability to read robot headlines critically.

Critical Thinking · BalancedRead it →
🚀The Singularity Is Nearer

By Ray Kurzweil (2024). The updated futurist vision — more specific on humanoids and embodied AI than any other book. Read it alongside AI Snake Oil for a balanced view of what's coming.

Futurist · Long-RangeRead it →
